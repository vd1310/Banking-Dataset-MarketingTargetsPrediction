Bank Marketing Analysis

Interactive Model

Background and Motivation

Every business has a limited marketing budget. Therefore it's vital that each dollar is spent in the most efficient way possible. In a bank's case, it's necessary to determine which of their clients will be receptive to phone marketing campaigns regarding the bank's financial services, specifically term deposits. Term deposits are similar to GICs, where a client will give the bank money in exchange for the money plus interest after a fixed period of time. During this time, the client is unable to withdraw their money.

The motivation behind the topic is to determine if marketing campaigns through phone calls is an effective use of marketing spend by companies such as a bank or large institution. This could also shed light on why so many people receive fraudulent phone calls of people claiming to be from IRS/CRA demanding money. If phone campaigns are truly effective, then one would expect to continue receiving fraudulent calls.

Analysis

There is no strong correlation aside from the relationship within a category such as job or past outcome variables. There is weak relationship between job and education as well as between marital status and age. This follows since only certain jobs are avaiable to those with a stronger education background. Additionally, marital status is typically changed the second quarter of life.

Results

Accuracy

88%

The most important features are the participants job, education and martial status. Past campaign participation also gives a strong indication of outcome.

Precision

52%

Recall

24%

While the precision and recall is poor, the model still serves as a meaningful filter for possible targets of a Term Deposit marketing campaign.
